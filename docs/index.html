Concept

What if you could know beforehand which trains are more prone to delays, crowdedness or other inconveniences? Based on your personal preferences you could easily pick whichever train suits your needs the most. Guess what: that’s exactly what #OASIS_team at #oSoc17 has done with their online application tripscore.

You can choose what makes commuting more enjoyable to you and then look at how suitable certain trains or buses are for you. It takes your personal preferences such as punctuality, frequency and the waiting time between trains into account. Then evaluates trips and gives them a score showing each aspect that contributed to the whole trip. Now you can start comparing trips to find the most suitable for you.

How can we make this dream come true? Open Data! The more data is published by your public transport providers, the more quality of experience aspects (delays, fare prices, frequency, …) we can take into account. Using this data we match existing service qualities to your expectations and predict your tripscore so you can pick the right train, tram or bus.

Tripscore was created by team OASIS at open Summer of Code 2017, a 4-week summer programme that provides Belgian based students the training, network and support necessary to transform open innovation projects into powerful real-world services. During the month of July 2017, 40 students worked in teams on 10 different open innovation projects provided by partnering organisations, companies and governments. The goal is to complete these projects by the end of the programme and turn them into real-world products and services.

OASIS is a team working towards Open Applications for Semantically Interoperable Services. Together, Ghent and the region of Madrid have initiated an innovative action that will increase the accessibility of public services and public transport. To do this, they collaborate to publish linked open data.
